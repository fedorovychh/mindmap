**Syndrome: The Illusion of Accessibility**

**Vibecoding**. A term usually perceived as something light and effortless. We’ve grown accustomed to thinking it’s about realizing an idea the moment it strikes, where the new programming language is simply plain English. But behind this facade of "easy vibes" hides a fundamental problem.

Today, we are witnessing a situation akin to **giving a monkey a pencil**. GenAI tools have created the illusion that a technical background is no longer necessary. As a result, stores are flooded with cookie-cutter applications that solve no real-world problems. People "vibe" their code, publish apps, and wait for that $1,000,000 success, forgetting that behind a flashy term must stand a product—not just a collection of generated lines.

This massive surge of "junk" projects creates noise that **overshadows genuinely high-quality products** written with expertise. When anyone can hit a "Generate" button, the value of engineering thought is neutralized in the eyes of the masses, even though reality dictates otherwise.

Let’s look at the numbers: general statistics show that **90% of startups die**. It’s crucial to understand: this figure represents only those who "survived," not those who became successful. The percentage of real success after 3+ years is even more sobering. The situation with GenAI products at the end of 2025 is even worse—most remain unprofitable. Resources are being burned (a single prompt consumes as much energy as boiling a kettle), and the ROI for SaaS solutions from "vibecoders" is non-existent.

We have reached a point where the tool's accessibility has become a trap. The desire to create "without knowledge" stimulates the brain to generate aimless things that aren't driven by market demand. This isn't just technical spam—it’s the beginning of a **cognitive revolution**, where we must finally separate the process of "drawing with a pencil" from the actual creation of value.

---

**What the "Vibe" Doesn't Forgive**

The truth is that vibecoding in its current form is a tool that won't allow you to create something truly adequate and viable in the long run. It is perfect for rapidly building a high-quality **PoC (Proof of Concept)** to visualize an idea, but it is absolutely ruthless toward a lack of fundamental knowledge.

You can "vibe out" the logic and the interface, but the system will not forgive failures in **security** or a total lack of product design understanding. AI won’t make your project secure or architecturally stable by default if you don’t understand how these mechanisms work "under the hood." This is the glass ceiling: a fast, flashy start that instantly hits a wall of technical incompetence when attempting real-world scaling.

To create truly valuable things, we need a different kind of tool. One that helps build everything correctly **"by design,"** abstracting the complexity of product and architectural levels away from the human. Recall how operating systems once abstracted hardware interaction. You don’t need to understand processor physics to work with files.

We need a similar level of abstraction for product creation, where the system handles the technical integrity while the human handles the **meanings**. Vibecoding is just the beginning—the first, rather primitive link in this great transition.

---

**Cognitive Inequality of the New Era**

The future is the **automation of automation**. While walking through shops recently, I—as an absolute ambassador of the AI concept—suddenly realized: I find interacting with people during purchases unpleasant. I would be glad to have AI perform this work systematically and knowledgeably, while humans remain behind the scenes, configuring this interaction for perfect performance.

We see a clear sequence: **Human (Customer) > AI > Human (Configurator)**. This is what awaits the generation born in 2010+. The skill set for a typical manager or cashier is changing radically—they are becoming technical specialists. This is happening everywhere. Modern neurosurgeons integrating brain modules are no longer just doctors; they are highly qualified tech specialists. We are entering the era of **Homo Deus**, where every profession is a technical specialty.

But how does this correlate with the "vibecoding" of non-technical people? Here lies the main threat. The desire to create without any knowledge of the processes alters one's consciousness, stimulating the brain to waste immense human resources on aimless things. The chasm between technical pros and non-technical vibecoders will have a sharply negative impact on society.

**Class inequality is evolving into cognitive inequality.** The need for technical specialists is growing as fast as the obsolescence of those who refuse to dive into the essence of things. The desire to create without learning leads to despair, psychological disorders, and financial ruin. Products assembled "without thought" inevitably run into legal risks and data privacy issues.

We are on the verge of an ethical catastrophe. The old model of "Knowledge > Work > Responsibility" is being replaced by "Request > Receipt > Launch > 'It wasn't me, it was the AI'." Responsibility becomes impossible to localize. In a world where everyone is a technician, vibecoders without knowledge become the most vulnerable class, risking being left on the sidelines of this cognitive revolution.

**From Programmer to Agentic Engineer**

Is the transition from programmers to AI agents really that bleak? Let’s consider the factors without which this process is simply impossible.

First, **quality control**. Large companies didn't spend years building complex systems only to let an agent uncontrollably edit "sacred" production code. That is a direct path to "hardcoding" and the destruction of programming paradigms. Without human oversight, automation quickly turns into technical chaos.

Second, **setup and deployment**. In the future, entire platforms will emerge to provide services for installing agents for specific goals—be it rapid feature delivery or system maintenance. **Agentic Engineers** will work on these. They are the next generation of engineers who look at the product globally. They understand not just frameworks, but architecture, planning, and delivery processes. They are the configurators of the result.

Third, **technical support**. Agents are prone to hallucinations. One wrong link in a chain of changes is enough to crash specific modules or the entire system. To identify the cause and fix the consequences, human involvement remains mandatory. Agents are dependent on external factors, whereas humans remain free in their decision-making.

Yes, the emergence of specialists managing agents will accelerate development severalfold. But in parallel, new technical roles are appearing where the focus shifts to deep knowledge: Machine Learning, hardware, and cloud infrastructure. Data centers aren't eternal, and unique ideas and innovations still require human intelligence. We aren't disappearing as a class—we are evolving into **architects** who manage this new power.

---

**From Syntax to the Architecture of Meaning**

Vibecoding is not the final destination; it is merely the cradle from which we must grow. It is the first, often naive link in an evolution where the English language becomes a bridge to implementation, but it will never replace intelligence. Right now, we see the "wild" phase: chaos, a lack of responsibility, and a glut of empty projects that exist for the sake of existing. But true value will appear when this experience transforms into a full-fledged system of abstraction.

We must realize: this is not a "replacement of programmers by AI," but a massive shift of focus toward global and conceptual knowledge. The technical specialist of the future no longer spends time writing syntax—they **design intent**. This is the transition from digital craftsmanship to **architectural sovereignty**. In this new world, diving into Machine Learning, understanding hardware logic, and mastering cloud architectures is no longer an "option for the elite"—it is a basic condition for survival.

If previously a programmer had to understand how computer memory works to write an efficient algorithm, now a technical specialist must understand how a cognitive model works to correctly configure a chain of autonomous agents. This is the very **automation of automation** I spoke of. Those who know the details of the process will be able to build the superstructure. Those who merely "vibe" without diving into the essence will forever remain consumers of others' abstractions, unable to move beyond a simple prototype.

This transition stage is a harsh cognitive filter. It will weed out those looking for easy paths without responsibility and bring a new caste of professionals to the forefront. We are heading into an era of working with upgrades, where the human idea is the primary driver and AI agents are the flawless executors. We are on the way to creating systems that will be as invisible and effective as modern operating systems, finally allowing us to focus on solving real problems rather than fighting with code.

Vibecoding gave us the tool to start. Now, it is our duty to learn how to build something of value with it.
